About Zogno

A local newspaper since 2020

Zogno is an Italian town of 8 617 inhabitants in the province of Bergamo in Lombardy.
The municipality is located in the lower Brembana Valley on a slope between the eastern
slope of Monte Zucco (1232 m a.s.l.) and a bend in the Brembo river.

Geography

Located in the lower portion of the Brembana Valley not far from San Pellegrino Terme (4 km),
the capital extends along the valley road, on the right orographic bank of the Brembo river,
while the other inhabited centers are mainly located on the left bank. The town is also located
at the foot of the north side of Canto Alto and the eastern side of Monte Zucco.

History

The area where the town stands was inhabited since prehistoric times, as evidenced by the finds
found in a local cave called "Büsa dell'Andrea".
There are documents dating back to the end of the 12th century that testify to the existence
of Zogno as a rural municipality: in the History of Zogno and some neighboring lands of Bortolo
Belotti we read that a parchment of the Monastery of Astino from 1220 mentions Giovanni Geze as "Consul" of Zogno [7].
Zogno became an important center under the domination of Venice. In past centuries the population
was mainly employed in agriculture, livestock breeding and pastoralism, although there was no lack
of handicraft activities related to the spinning of wool, linen and the weaving of cloths.
In the hamlet of Poscante on 18 December 1773 the famous brigand Vincenzo Pacchiana (Pacì Paciana) was born,
an Italian Robin Hood who stole from the rich and gave to the poor, called by his contemporaries: king of the road,
king of the mountains. During the 19th century, the paper and cement industries took hold. Today, thanks to constant
development, both in the economic field (important for example the Manifattura Valle Brembana, a textile industry
that still exists today, which has recently failed) and urban planning, Zogno is the most populous town in the
brembana Valley with numerous commercial activities, textiles, mining, wood crafts and construction companies.

Monuments and places of interest


  • Parish church of San Lorenzo
    • In a dominant position stands the fifteenth-century church of San Lorenzo, built on the ruins of the medieval
    castle of the Guelph side, destroyed by the Visconti before handing it over to the Republic of Venice,
    after the peace of Lodi. The interior houses the seventeenth-century series of the Mysteries of the Rosary
    by Francesco Zucco, a Madonna and Child by Francesco Lavagna and a painting by Vincenzo Angelo Orelli
    depicting Santa Margherita da Cortona.
  • Museum
    • The Museum of the Valley, located in a seventeenth-century building in the historic center,
    exhibits a rich series of finds on the agricultural and peasant society of the valley in twelve rooms.
  • San Lorenzo Museum
    • Also worth mentioning is the Museum of San Lorenzo, set up in the parish church,
    which collects evidence of a religious nature from the Brembana Valley.
  • Villa Belotti
    • The Zognese villa, which has housed the "Bortolo Belotti" Municipal Library since 1985,
    was built in 1906 for the notary Ulisse Cacciamali by the Bergamo architect Giovanni Barboglio,
    author in Zogno also of public buildings and the old staircase to the parish church, but more
    famous in Lombardy for the construction or restoration of churches.
